About

  • About Parole ME

    Who we are and what we stand for. . .

    Parole ME is a multi-organizational collaborative advocacy initiative to engage members of Maine communities to raise awareness, educate, and engage in mindful public discussion about the reenactment of parole in the State of Maine.

  • April 16 1976

    Parole Abolished Maine

    Tough-on-Crime and Truth-in-Sentencing political ideologies prompted Governor James Longely to sign a bill for the abolition of parole in Maine. Maine was the first state to introduce statutory sentencing reforms that transitioned from indetermintate to determinant sentencing structures. Individuals sentences after April 1976 are no longer eligible for Board review of their personal growth, rehabilitation, or carceral status.

  • March 21, 2022

    Resolve passed to form Parole Study Commission

    The Senate unanimously passed a resolve to form a legislative commission to study reenacting parole in Maine's criminal justice system. This resolve passed into law without the governor's signature.

  • September 8, 2022

    The Parole Study Commission will covene their first meeting to research and discuss the benefits and risks of reinstating parole in the State of Maine.

    The Parole Study Commission will assess the impact of parole reenactment from September - December before submitting a report of their recommendations and proposed policies to the legislature.

Rehabilitative Gardening Program

Personal Growth, Food Security, and Holistic Care for All!

Incarcerated populations are disproportionately impacted by cycles of poverty and inequitable access to healthcare. Through horticultural and botanical education that vocationally trains Corrections residents in agricultural production and medicinal food science, we can pave career paths for residential release and share the fruits of our bounty as a community service to combat food insecurity for fellow Mainers.

Colby College and The Maine Prisoner Advocacy Coalition present. . .

  • 67.8% of justice-involved individuals are rearrested within 3 years post-release.

  • 76.6% are rearrested within 5 years.

    • According to a 2014 study conducted by Rand Corp, corrections educational programming lowered recidivism rates by 43% and raised levels of successful employment by 12%
